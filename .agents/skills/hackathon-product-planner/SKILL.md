# Hackathon Product Planner

Plan the smallest compelling product that demonstrates a credible Delhi beauty marketplace and makes the AI value obvious to judges.

## Workflow

1. State the target user, their Delhi-specific problem, and the product promise.
2. Map the shortest complete journey from discovery to salon selection or booking.
3. Connect each proposed feature to one or more judging criteria.
4. Classify every feature as:
   - **Must-have:** Required for the core journey or judging story.
   - **Nice-to-have:** Valuable only after the core demo is reliable and polished.
   - **Avoid for hackathon:** High effort, low demo value, duplicative, or risky.
5. Define the demo narrative, including the moment where AI produces visible value.
6. Identify assumptions, dependencies, and the fastest validation method.

## Decision Rules

- Prefer one polished user journey over several partial journeys.
- Keep the marketplace specific to Delhi localities, customer needs, and salon context.
- Make the startup positioning clear in the hero, core workflow, and final call to action.
- Reject features that cannot be explained or demonstrated quickly.
- Preserve a reliable fallback path for external services.

## Output

Return a concise plan containing the product promise, primary persona, core journey, feature classification, judging-criteria mapping, demo sequence, and top risks.
